Tiramisu Pots

Serves 6

For this cheats's tiramisu, I used condenced milk in a mascarpone cream rather than eggs, which would be more tradtional but also more time-consuming. The milk brings a silky sweetness that goes beautifly with the chocolate, and it still ticks all the boxes for texture and flavor. If you're entertaining, make these ahead of time and leave them in the fridge until you are ready to serve.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cold black coffee
  • 1/4 cup UV Chocolate Cake Vodka
  • 1/4 cup coffe liqueur
  • 18 ladyfigures
  • 1 1/2 ounces dark chocolate (80% cocoa solids), for grating

For the mascarpone cream

  • 9 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup condenced milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup Marsala wine

Instructions

  1. Pour the coffee into a shallow tray ans mix in the coffee liqueur. Carefully place the tray in the freezer.

  2. Meanwhile, put the mascarpone and condenced milk into a medium bowl and whisk until smooth.

  3. Put the cream into a separate bowl and beat with a handheld electric mixer until firm peaks form. Stir in the Marsala, then fold the cream into mascarpone mixture.

  4. Remove the coffee from the freezer. If it's still not cold, add a couple of ice cubes to the tray and stir until cool. Take 9 of the ladyfigures and soak them one at a time in the coffee for a few seconds. Break the soaked ladyfingures in half and divide them amoung the bottoms of the six serving glasses.

  5. Using a fine grater and half of the chocolate, grate a layer of chocolate directly over the ladyfingres. Spoon half of the mascarpone mixture amoung the glasses.

  6. Dip the remaining ladyfingures into the coffee one at a time. Break them in half and divide them amoung the glasses. Grate the rest of the chocolate over the ladyfingers, then top each glass with the remaining mascarpone mixture.

  7. Before serving, use a small tea strainer to dust cocoa powder over the surface.

Bon Appetit!
